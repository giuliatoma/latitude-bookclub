I started this club because Berlin is full of serious readers — and I couldn't find them. After living across five countries and spending years moving between cultures, the books I've loved most are the ones that took me somewhere I'd never been and taught me something true.

I read fiction the way most people read non-fiction: to understand how the world works, why people do what they do, and what it actually feels like to live inside a history you've only read about in headlines. A novel about the one-child policy in rural China or four generations of Koreans in Japan can teach you something no textbook can.

This club is for people who feel the same way — curious adults who take fiction seriously, who aren't looking for easy reads or trending titles, and who want the kind of conversation you can only have when everyone in the room has read the same book.

The Format

One book. One country. One evening.

Every month we read a novel or narrative non-fiction set in a different country or culture. Historical fiction, political memoir, diaspora stories — books about war, education, identity, womanhood, revolution. Always somewhere new. Always something that earns its themes rather than performing them.

We meet in a cozy Berlin café on a weekday evening. Everyone orders their own drink. The conversation runs for about two hours. No prep required beyond finishing the book.

The opening trilogy

For the first three sessions I choose the book — short enough to finish in a busy month, serious enough to set the right tone. These three establish what this club is about before the group starts voting.

01
Albania · 274 pages · Memoir
Free
Lea Ypi
Communism Political awakening Education Coming-of-age

Growing up in Stalinist Albania and watching it collapse overnight. Ypi is a political philosopher at LSE — this is memoir as intellectual awakening. An ideal opener because it sets the club's ambitions immediately: serious, political, personal, and from a country most people know almost nothing about.

02
Mexico · 192 pages · Literary fiction
Stillborn
Guadalupe Nettel
Motherhood Female friendship Choice Illness

Short, precise and quietly devastating. Two women making different choices about motherhood. Moves the geography to Latin America early and surprises people who expect literary fiction to be slow. Easy to finish, hard to forget.

03
USA · 352 pages · Memoir
Educated
Tara Westover
Education Feminism Family Power

A woman who never attended school becomes a Cambridge historian. About who controls what you are allowed to know, and what it costs to leave the world you were born into. Gripping enough that everyone finishes it — important in session 3 when the group is still forming.

Session 4 onwards, group-voted
